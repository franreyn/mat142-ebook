Apply Problem Solving Process and Strategies

In Chapter 0 you were introduced to some fundamental ideas for problems solving strategies based on the work from Polya. Below is a quick summary.

Problem Solving Process

  1. Understand the Problem
  2. Devise a Plan
  3. Carry out the Plan
  4. Look Back

Let us now look at applications that take into consideration the topics we covered in this course.

Example 1

Joseph is remoldeling a kitchen and received a quote for installation and labor of a new floor to be $105 per sq yard (this includes taxes). The kitchen has dimensions of 22 ft 5 in by 15 ft. In addition Jospeh will purchase a new Refridgerator for $2,900, range for $1,300, and a dishwasher for $899 at a local store (where sales tax is 8.7%).

Solution

Joseph needs to finance this remodeling project. At the local bank he can get a line of credit with an annual rate of 7% with monthly payments with a 5 year term. Determine the total cost of the remodel, the payments for the loan, the cost of the loan, and the total cost of the remodel if payments are made as scheduled.

The problem above illustrates what really can be involved as we look at a general application of material you have worked on. We needed an understanding of how to convert units, find the area, determines sales tax, and find the payment amount on an amortized loan.

Exercises

In the next 4 questions, estimate the values by making reasonable approximations for unknown values, or by doing some research to find reasonable values.

  1. Estimate how many gallons of water you drink in a year.
    Answer

    An answer around 100-300 gallons would be reasonable.

  2. Estimate how many times you blink in a day.
  3. How long would it take to fill a standard olympic sized pool with a garden hose (50 m x 25 m x 3 m)?
  4. How many gallons of paint would be needed to paint a two-story house 40 ft long and 30 ft wide?
  5. During the landing of the Mars Science Laboratory Curiosity, it was reported that the signal from the rover would take 14 minutes to reach earth.  Radio signals travel at the speed of light, about 186,000 miles per second.  How far was Mars from Earth when Curiosity landed?
    Answer

    A156 million miles.

  6. It is estimated that a driver takes, on average, 1.5 seconds from seeing an obstacle to reacting by applying the brake or swerving.  How far will a car traveling at 60 miles per hour travel (in feet) before the driver reacts to an obstacle?
    Answer

    132 ft.

  7. Sound travels about 750 miles per hour.  If you stand in a parking lot near a building and sound a horn, you will hear an echo. 
    1. Suppose it takes about ½ a second to hear the echo.  How far away is the building1?
    2. Now let’s generalize that result.  Suppose it takes n seconds to hear the echo.  How far away is the building, in terms of n?
  8. A box of Jell-O costs $0.50, and makes 2 cups.  How much would it cost to fill a swimming pool 4 feet deep, 8 feet wide, and 12 feet long with Jell-O? (1 cubic foot is about 7.5 gallons and there are 16 cups per gallon)
  9. Soda is often sold in 20 ounce bottles.  The nutrition label for one of these bottles is shown to the right.  A packet of sugar (the kind they have at restaurants for your coffee or tea) typically contain 4 grams of sugar in the U.S.  Drinking a 20 oz soda is equivalent to eating how many packets of sugar?2
    Answer

    There are 2.5 servings in the container and each serving has 30 grams of sugar. This gives a total of 2.5*30=75 grams of sugar for a 20oz soda. A quick google search and you will find a packet of sugar has 4 grams of sugar. The 20 oz soda has 75 g r a m s 4 g r a m / p a c k e t = 18.75 p a c k e t s of sugar.

  10. Brandon walked from Rincon/UHS to UofA. It took him 1 hour and 35 minutes. He then took 45 minutes to walk from UofA to Pima Downtown Campus. If he did not stop at any point and arrived at the Pima Downtown Campus at 4:25 pm, then what time did he leave Rincon/UHS?
  11. Abby is not a morning person and needs to plan on what time to wake up for a morning class. She needs 30 minutes to get ready in the morning. It takes her 25 minutes to bike to campus and walk to class. If her class starts at 8:45 a.m. what time should she set her alarm for?
